# Western Canon

The **Western canon** refers to the body of classic works of literature, philosophy, and art that have been traditionally considered essential for a well-rounded education in Western societies.

## Importance in Alternative Education

Critics of modern public education argue that the Western canon has been marginalized in curricula in favor of more contemporary or identity-focused content. Proponents believe:

- It provides timeless wisdom and cultural literacy
- Works by authors like Aristotle, Shakespeare, and Orwell develop critical thinking
- Understanding the canon is key to understanding Western civilization and its values

## Educated and Free's Book List

The newsletter 'A Library to Build Great Americans' offers over 400 books in the Western canon, separated by age groups. This resource aims to help parents provide a traditional education outside schools.

## Debates

- **Defenders:** The canon represents the best of human thought and should be central to education
- **Critics:** It is exclusionary, Eurocentric, and ignores diverse perspectives
- **Pragmatists:** A balanced curriculum includes both canonical works and diverse voices
