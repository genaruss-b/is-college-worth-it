# College vs Alternative Pathways

This page compares traditional four-year college education with alternative pathways such as homeschooling, classical education, trade schools, and self-directed learning.

## Dimensions of Comparison

| Dimension | Traditional College | Alternative Pathways |
|-----------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Cost | High tuition, potential debt | Often lower cost, minimal debt |
| Time | 4 years typical | Variable, often shorter |
| Curriculum | Set by institution | Customizable by parent/student |
| Socialization | Campus life, peers | Family/community based |
| Outcomes | Degree, network | Portfolio, skills, certifications |

## Perspectives

Critics like [Dissident Teacher](entity/dissident-teacher.md) argue that alternatives can provide superior education without ideological contamination. Proponents of college point to research showing higher lifetime earnings and lower unemployment for graduates.

## Need for More Data

This comparison requires empirical evidence on outcomes for alternative-educated students. Current discourse is largely ideological rather than evidence-based.
